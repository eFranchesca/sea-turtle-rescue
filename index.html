Our Oceans Need our Help!

Established in 1969 Oceanic Society was the first non-profit organization in America dedicated to marine conservation. Today their mission is to improve ocean health by deepening the connections between people and nature to address the root cause of its decline—human behavior!

Oceanic Society History

Oceanic Society was founded in 1969 by a group of San Francisco Bay Area sailors and scientists who were concerned about the state of the oceans and decided to take action. Inspired by the environmental awakening of the 1960s and further motivated by events like the Santa Barbara oil spill of 1969, they established Oceanic Society.

    2022 Global Ocean Cleanup

    On September 17, 2022, Oceanic Society teamed up with organizations worldwide to clean up some of Earth’s most important coastal and ocean habitats. More than 400 volunteers joined cleanups in Guatemala, Hawaii (USA), India, Kenya, Miami (USA), Palau, San Francisco (USA), and Spain, collectively removing more than 4,598 lbs of trash from the environment. Cleanup locations included sea turtle nesting beaches, albatross habitat, mangrove forests, and coastlines adjacent to sensitive coral reefs and marine mammal hotspots.
